CHIEF COMPLAINT:
Suspicious mole on the back.

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:
The patient is a 34-year-old male with no significant past medical history who presents today for evaluation of a new mole on his upper back. He states his wife noticed it a few months ago and believes it has become darker and slightly larger. He denies any itching, bleeding, or pain associated with the lesion. No personal or family history of melanoma. 

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION:
Skin: On the right upper back, lateral to the scapula, there is a 6mm asymmetric, irregularly bordered macule with variegation in color (dark brown and black). The rest of a full body skin exam reveals no other suspicious lesions. Normal nevi are present on the anterior trunk and extremities.

PROCEDURE:
After discussing the risks and benefits, informed consent was obtained for an excisional biopsy of the lesion. The area was prepped and draped in standard sterile fashion and anesthetized with 1% lidocaine with epinephrine. A tangential shave excision was performed, yielding a 7mm specimen which was sent to pathology. Hemostasis was achieved. A bandage was applied. Patient tolerated the procedure well.

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
1. Dysplastic nevus vs. malignant melanoma of the back. Excisional biopsy performed. Will await pathology results and call the patient.
2. Discussed sun safety and regular self skin exams.
