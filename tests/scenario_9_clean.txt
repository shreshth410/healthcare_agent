CHIEF COMPLAINT:
Headaches.

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:
The patient is a 42-year-old male with a history of recurrent headaches over the last 5 years. He describes them as bilateral, non-throbbing, mild-to-moderate band-like tightness around his head. They occur mostly toward the end of his workday, typically 2-3 times a week. He denies any photophobia, phonophobia, nausea, or vomiting. He states the pain does not worsen with routine physical activity. He takes over-the-counter acetaminophen with mild relief.

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY:
1. Allergic rhinitis.
2. Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD).

MEDICATIONS:
1. Flonase nasal spray, 1 spray each nostril daily.
2. Omeprazole 20mg daily.
3. Acetaminophen 500mg PRN for headaches.

PHYSICAL AND NEUROLOGICAL EXAMINATION:
General: Alert and oriented x3, in no acute distress.
Cranial Nerves: II-XII intact.
Motor: 5/5 strength throughout all upper and lower extremities.
Sensory: Intact to light touch and pinprick symmetrically.
Reflexes: 2+ and symmetric in biceps, triceps, patellar, and Achilles. Babinski sign is negative bilaterally.
Gait: Normal, stable station.
Cerebellar: Normal finger-to-nose and heel-to-shin testing.

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
1. Tension-type headaches. The patient's history is classic for frequent episodic tension-type headaches, lacking migrainous features or red flags. Neurological examination is completely normal.
2. Given the frequency, recommend starting a daily preventive medication. Initiated Amitriptyline 10mg at bedtime. Explained possible side effects like sedation and dry mouth.
3. Advised on stress management techniques and consistent sleep hygiene. 
Follow up in neurology clinic in 3 months.
