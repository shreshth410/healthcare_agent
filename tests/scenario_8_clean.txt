CHIEF COMPLAINT:
Abdominal pain and diarrhea.

HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS:
The patient is a 28-year-old female presenting to the gastroenterology clinic for evaluation of chronic abdominal pain and diarrhea. She notes the symptoms began insidiously about 6 months ago, mostly crampy lower abdominal pain relieved somewhat by defecation. She is having 4-5 loose to watery bowel movements a day. She denies bloody stools or fever. 

PAST MEDICAL HISTORY:
1. Migraines, occasionally relieved by Sumatriptan.
2. Anxiety, currently well controlled.

MEDICATIONS:
1. Lexapro 10mg daily.
2. Sumatriptan 50mg PRN.

PHYSICAL EXAMINATION:
Vitals: BP 118/74, HR 72, RR 16, Temp 98.4 F, Wt 130 lbs.
General: Well appearing, alert, and oriented.
Abdomen: Soft, non-distended. Mild tenderness to deep palpation in the bilateral lower quadrants. No rebound or guarding. Bowel sounds are normal.
Rectal Exam: Normal sphincter tone. No masses or hemorrhoids appreciated. Hemoccult test is negative for occult blood.

LABORATORY AND DIAGNOSTIC RESULTS:
- CBC: WBC 7.2, Hgb 13.8 (normal), Plt 280
- BMP: All values within normal limits. 
- CRP: Normal
- Tissue Transglutaminase IgA (tTG-IgA): Negative
- Stool cultures (C. diff, Salmonella, Shigella, Campylobacter): Negative
- Colonoscopy performed two weeks ago showed normal colonic mucosa with random biopsies returning normal histology.

ASSESSMENT AND PLAN:
1. Irritable Bowel Syndrome with Diarrhea (IBS-D). Symptoms and negative workup are consistent with this diagnosis. Discussed the diagnosis, pathophysiology, and natural history with the patient.
2. Recommend symptom management with Loperamide 2mg as needed for diarrhea.
3. Will initiate a trial of Dicyclomine 20mg four times a day as needed for severe abdominal cramping.
4. Referral to a dietitian for a low-FODMAP diet education.
Follow-up in 8 weeks to assess progress.
